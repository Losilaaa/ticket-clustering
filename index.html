The hidden cost

The problem isn't the ticket.
It's the bouncing.

When a ticket isn't routed right the first time, it doesn't just take longer. It absorbs time from multiple people, loses context at every handoff, and makes the user re-explain the problem from scratch. That overhead is invisible in most dashboards.

The context tax nobody measures

Every reassignment adds roughly 15 minutes of re-reading overhead for the receiving team, before they even start fixing. Two reassignments means 30 minutes of pure overhead per ticket. Multiply that by 40% of your P2 volume, over 12 months. It adds up fast.

The approach

You can't resolve
what you can't see.

The answer is not a new ITSM platform or a faster L1 team. It's surfacing the 4–5 recurring patterns that are generating most of the volume. Cluster the history, build routing rules, automate the top patterns. In that order.

01

Cluster your last 90 days of tickets

Run your ticket history through a topic model. Four or five dominant patterns will surface: the same problems recurring across sites and teams. This takes hours, not months. The data is already there.

Output: the short list that's driving most of your volume
02

Build routing rules for each cluster

Not AI magic. Simple, auditable rules tied to each pattern. The right team receives the ticket the first time. Reassignments drop immediately. No process redesign required. You're just making the invisible visible.

Output: misrouting rate drops 60–80% for the covered patterns
03

Automate the top 2–3 patterns

The ones with known runbooks and clear resolution steps. Zero-touch resolution for the most common issues. This is where MTTR moves. Not for every ticket. For the specific clusters where the fix is already documented.

Output: 15–30% of total volume resolved without human triage

Why this isn't a 6-month project

You already have the data. Every ticket ever raised in your ITSM is training material. Clustering it takes a week, not a quarter. The bottleneck is knowing what to look for, not how long it takes to find it.
